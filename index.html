Advocating for Improved Prenatal and Postnatal Care for Culturally Diverse Women in New Zealand

ABOUT US

Welcome to MAMA, the Maternal Health Collective dedicated to advocating for improving Maternal Health in Aotearoa.

Our Mission

We are committed to advocating for improved prenatal and postnatal care for culturally diverse women in New Zealand. Join us as we address barriers and enhance support systems for women across the country.

MAMA SPEAKS

Here, we amplify the voices of mothers who have navigated the complexities of the New Zealand health system firsthand. Listen to their experiences, challenges, and invaluable insights as we collectively strive for positive transformation in maternal health care.

Featured Conversations

  • Listen as Cynthia (not her real name), a 39-year-old mother of four, shares her journey through three traumatising births and her interactions with the New Zealand health system. She sheds light on the hurdles she faced and the changes she envisions.



    “I was scared. I really struggled”
    “Finding a midwife was hard, imagine if English wasn’t my first language”
    “I never saw my first midwife in-person"
    “I was traumatised, I was bleeding in my 5th week and my midwife wasn’t responding”
    “The scan system wouldn’t tell me anything, I was told to let my body do what it needs to do – while bleeding!”
    “I had to keep travelling to get my scans done and I couldn’t drive”
    “The Doctors wanted to control my body; they wouldn’t let go”
    “Everything was just wrong, random people were touching me”
    “I got woken up at 1 am, after a hemorrhage and told ‘I need to go’, they need to change the bed, right after just having a twin birth!”
